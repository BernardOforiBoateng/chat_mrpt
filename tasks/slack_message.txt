THE ISSUE:
Grace's DBI and GHSL values are fractions (0-1) instead of percentages (0-100%). She needs to multiply by 100.

THE FIX:
In her buffer calculation script, lines 93 and 102, change:

var ghslValue = safeGetNumber(ghslDict, 'ghsl');
var dbiValue = safeGetNumber(dbiDict, 'dbi');

To:

var ghslValue = safeGetNumber(ghslDict, 'ghsl').multiply(100);
var dbiValue = safeGetNumber(dbiDict, 'dbi').multiply(100);

WHY DBI VALUES ARE LOW (from my original report):
DBI is designed for arid regions and is intentionally conservative. For Delta State:
- DBI mean 5.4% aligns with Delta's known rural nature
- DBI outperforms NDBI which overestimates at 33.1%
- DBI has better correlation with MODIS (r=0.839 vs NDBI's r=0.676)
- Low values are EXPECTED and CORRECT for non-arid, rural areas like Delta State

For arid northern Nigeria, DBI values will be even lower (0-2%), which is appropriate since those areas are extremely rural.
